About Me

A passion for technology

As a young kid, I would stack phone books on the computer desk chair so I could reach my families spare computer to create designs with Microsoft Paint. In middle school, I would build and decorate houses on The Sims, learning a lot about color theory. In college, I studied abroad in Buenos Aires, Argentina and became fascinated with European style architecture. I desperately wanted to capture the unique facades through photography and used basic photo edits to transform the images. My passion for photography grew and I began capturing the beauty of daily life and became comfortable editing photos for hours at a time.

A passion for understanding people

Throughout my college years, I spent much time in the restaurant industry waiting tables and creating experiences for guests. I had to identify who they were and what they wanted to enhance their dining experience according to their unique needs. Having numerous guests at the same time, I learned the importance of staying calm during stressful situations and how to multitask.
Following graduation, I spent time in sales and customer service learning empathy, communication, and listening skills. In both positions, it was important to discover user needs and propose a solution that solved their problems.

Why I became a designer

Throughout my life, I have depended on web/mobile based technologies to perform daily tasks. I have struggled with poorly structured interfaces, module windows that disrupt workflow, module windows with no clear escape and icons with no labels. These examples are only some of the problems that I have experienced with poorly designed products. These struggles made me think… “hey, I can be a better designer; someone who designs with the user needs first and respects those needs to create a product that the user enjoys using”.
After that moment of clarity, I realized that user-centered design was the right path for me. My passion for technology and understanding people paired well with my eye for design and love for creating beautiful things. Additionally, while attending University I studied research methods for my Bachelors of Science degree in Communications and fell in love with analyzing data. These passions combined were all skills that a UX Designer needed to succeed, bringing me to embark on the path of becoming a user-focused designer.

My hobbies

Outside of design, I am passionate about maintaining a healthful lifestyle and I find much enjoyment in healthy cooking, practicing yoga, and running on the beach. I love trying new things and learned how to surf and snowboard in the past few years. The resturaunt industry led me to appreciate the culinary arts and you can often find me seeking out thoughtfully crafted dishes, artisanal cheese plates and wine.
Creativity plays an important role in my life and I craft beautiful things in my spare time. Some of my favorite hobbies include capturing a beautiful facade or nature scene with my camera, learning how to sketch, melting wax with flowers to make a candle, creating driftwood art or jotting down a few lines of poetry as I am inspired throughout my daily life.
